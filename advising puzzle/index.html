Undergraduate Advising at Cal

How does advising work at Cal?

Who do I go to for what?

Why do I have so many advisors?

At times, UC Berkeley can feel like a large ecosystem as students are learning to navigate the university in many ways. There are various advising units on campus that have unique functions but sometimes can overlap. Advising is a critical support system where students can receive academic/personal resources, gather information, clarify policies, and seek solutions. Advisors are invested in your success and we are here to partner with you to help strategically maximize your time at UC Berkeley. This “advising puzzle” can assist you in learning about the many advising units on campus and how they can best meet your circumstantial needs.

college advisor
College advisors build relationships with students to understand their particular needs and focus on their academic & career goals. Their primary role is to support students with general program planning and academic advising while making appropriate referrals to student services, faculty and enrichment opportunities. College advisors also assist students in navigating college policies such as degree requirements, deadlines, unit limits, academic holds, withdrawals and more. If you are undeclared or already in a major, you might need to begin by exploring an area of interest. The next step is to identify a particular school or college where your academic goals can be met. To schedule an appointment with a college advisor, use the links below.

educational opportunity program (EOP)
The Educational Opportunity Program (EOP) serves undergraduate students who are first-generation, low-income, and/or underrepresented students. EOP Academic Counselors can support you with academic planning, financial assistance, graduate school / career exploration and building community at UC Berkeley. The quickest way to verify your EOP eligibility is by contact our front office at 510.642.7224 or visiting 119 Chavez. You can also apply online through our website below.

119 Cesar Chavez Student Center

Visit website
cal student central
Cal Student Central (CSC) is a one-stop resource that includes the Office of Financial Aid & Scholarships, Office of the Registrar and Billing & Payment Services. CSC helps students understand their student budget, financial aid package (loans, grants/scholarships and work-study), appeals processes, etc. Additionally, CSC assists students with billing/payment inquiries, transcripts, enrollment verifications, residency and more.

      peer advisors

      Peer Advisors are current students who use their personal experience to provide student-to-student advising and mentoring about several topics including:
      1. Academic planning & creating a balanced workload
      2. Research engagement
      3. Student life and leadership
      4. Community development

      Peer Advisors are trained to understand basic departmental policies and procedures and university requirements. They often assist departmental units with outreach and enrichment programs. Additionally, some Peer Advisors support with community engagement and leadership development. Various departments on campus have have peer advisor programs; be sure to contact your major department for peer advising inquiries.
